What is linguistic ambiguity?
Within human language, ambiguity occurs
when a word, phrase, or sentence has
more than one possible interpretation.
Humans resolve ambiguity naturally
using context. For machines, ambiguity
is a major challenge.
For an example, let's look at the
sentence I saw her duck.
This sentence can have multiple valid
meanings, like, does the sentence mean
that the subject or she was ducking?
Or does the sentence mean? That I saw
her duck the animal.
Now let's look at polysemy. Polygamy
refers to a single word having multiple
related meanings.
Polygamy is extremely common in natural
human language.
Let's look at some examples. The word
bank can mean a financial institution.
Or the bank of a river.
The word paper can mean the writing
material. Or an academic article.
The word run can have meanings like jog,
to manage, or a function.
For effective NLP systems. Word meaning
must be inferred from its context. And
therefore just the dictionary lookups
are insufficient.
Now let's look at synonymy.
Synonymy occurs when different word
express similar meanings. For example,
Big And large, they are different words,
but have similar meanings. Buy and
purchase, happy and joyful, they all
have similar meanings.
Synonymy causes challenges, such as
vocabulary sparsity.
And difficulty in matching semantically
similar text.
