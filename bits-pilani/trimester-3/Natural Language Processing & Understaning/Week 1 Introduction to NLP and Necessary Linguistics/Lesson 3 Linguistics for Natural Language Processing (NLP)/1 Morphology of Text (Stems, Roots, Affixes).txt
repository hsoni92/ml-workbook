What is morphology?
Morphology is the branch of linguistics
that studies the internal structure of
words and their relations.
Within NLP, morphology helps us
understand how words are formed, how
different words relate to each other.
And why words appear in multiple
variants. For machines, recognising
these relationships is critical for
reducing vocabulary size and improving
generalisation.
Why does morphology matter within NLP?
For 2 basic reasons. Morphology
directly motivates how the text should
be treated for machine processing.
And how the model looks at the text
data and their internal relationships.
