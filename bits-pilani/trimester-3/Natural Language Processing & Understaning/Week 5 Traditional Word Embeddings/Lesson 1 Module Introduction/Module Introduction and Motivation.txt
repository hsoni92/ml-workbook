Welcome learners to the 5th module on
traditional word embeddings.
In this module, we will explore how
word embeddings bridge the gap between
linguistic text and mathematical
computation by introducing. Fundamental
vectorization techniques. You will
learn to transform raw text into
numerical features using one hot
encoding, bag of words, TFIDF, and
ngrams, effectively preparing data for
machine learning algorithms while
understanding the trade-offs between
sparsity and semantic retention.
But why do we need vectorization?
Understand that NLP models are
mathematical engines, they cannot read
text like humans do, they only
understand numbers, specifically zeros
and ones.
To feed text data into a model, we must
translate the text into a numerical
format that the machine can understand.
This process is called vectorization or
word embedding.
We started with simple rule based
approaches, such as rejects and
linguistics, like stemming and
lematization.
Now we enter the era of statistical NLP,
where we convert words into vectors to
perform mathematical operations on them.
