phase to organise those billions of
results. Now we arrive at step 4, the
reduce phase. This is the moment where
all our parallel stream of data finally
meet to produce the definitive answers
the business is looking for. As you can
see on the slide. The reducer phase is
the 3rd and final logical stage of the
map reduce job. Its primary goal is to
consolidate the results from the
shuffle and shod phase. This phase is
the functional core of the entire model.
It is rooted in the mathematical
concept of fold or reduce. If you
imagine our data as a long unfolded
piece of paper representing every
single click on a website, the reduced
phase folds that paper down until only
the total sum remains. It takes the
organised piles created during the
shuffle and turns them into a single,
summarised output folder. Now let's
look at how this work physically on
next slide. The aggregation funnel. In
this phase, each reducer node acts like
a funnel. It receives a key and a list
of all the values associated with that
key. For example, the key might be
index or estimate, and the list might
be 1, 1,111, and so on, representing
every time someone visited that page.
The developer's job here is simple
write the logic that aggregates the
list. Usually this is an arithmetic sum,
but it could be an average. It could be
maximum value or a philtre. The
operation can become mathematically
complex depending upon the business use
case, because a reducer has a complete
view of every instance of that key
across the entire cluster. The result
it produces is the absolute global
truth. The most important takeaway here
is data reduction. Think back to over a
10 terabyte log file in the map phase.
We were still dealing with 10 terabytes
of data, but in the reduced phase we
are performing a massive compression of
information. We're turning the noise of
billions of individual events into the
signal a few megabytes of ranked
insights. Once the reducer finishes its
calculations, it writes the final
results directly to the distributed
file system, the job is now complete,
and the data is ready for the business
analyst to read.
Just to recap, our entire logical data

Shuffle and sort, organises and moves
