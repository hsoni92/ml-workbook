In module one, we learned that scaling
out was the only way to handle big data,
but this week we looked behind the
curtain and realised that scaling out
comes with a complex price. We move
from building the fleet of vans to
learning how to keep all those drivers
coordinated when the roads are blocked
and the radios are failing.
Our first major takeaway was that the
network is not a perfect wire. We
deconstructed the fallacies of
distributed computing, and learned that
as an architect, you must never assume
the network is reliable, that the
latency is zero. Or that the bandwidth
is infinite. We learned that these
assumptions are what cause big data
projects to fail in the real world. But
by adopting a design for failure
mindset, we stop treating network
glitches as accidents and start
treating them as guarantees. This is
the foundation of partition tolerance,
the absolute requirement that our
system stays alive even when
communication breaks down.
Next we mastered the famous rule in our
field, the Cap Theorem. Because
partition tolerance is mandatory in a
cluster, we learned that we are forced
into permanent tradeoff between
consistency and availability.
We saw that CP systems, like banking,
choose to be correct or offline. We saw
that AP systems, like social media,
choose to be fast and always on, even
if the data is slightly stale.
Understanding that you cannot have all
three at once. Is that mark of a
professional. Your job is no longer to
find a perfect system, but to find the
right trade-off for your specific
business problem. Finally, we looked at
how these theories are put into
practise through consistency models. We
reviewed acid, the strict golden
standard that ensures every transaction
is atomic and isolated. It's the anchor
of the financial world.
Then we met its distributed sibling
base. We learned that by accepting
basically available and eventual
consistency, we can build systems that
scale to millions of users.
Something that is physically impossible.
With a strict acid approach.
Knowing when to demand the hard truth
of asset versus the flowing truth of
base. Is one of the most valuable
skills you now possess. You now have a
deep understanding of the challenge of
distributed systems. You know that
coordination is the real network tax of
big data. Great job. Happy learning.
I'll see you in the next module. Thank
you.
