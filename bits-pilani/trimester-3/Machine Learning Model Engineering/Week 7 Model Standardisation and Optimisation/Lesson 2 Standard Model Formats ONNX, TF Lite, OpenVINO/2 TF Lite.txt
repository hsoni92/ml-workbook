First up, ONNX which stands for Open
Neural Network Exchange. OneNRX is an
open standard for representing neural
network models. Many frameworks can
export to ONNX, Pytorch, Tensorflow,
even traditional machine learning model
via converters. Once you have a .OX
file, you can run it with ONNX runtime,
or import it into tools like TensorRT.
ONNX shines when you care about
interoperability between multiple
frameworks and deployment stacks.
And general purpose servings on servers
and cloud platforms. That's why we will
use ONNX in the Model 7 labs. It's a
very practical default format for
production systems. Next in Tensorflow
light.
Often is shorthand to TF Lite. TF Lite
is both a model format,
TF Light files, and a runtime focused
on mobile and embedded devices. It's
designed to have a small binary size,
use optimised kernels for mobile CPUs
and NPUs, and work well with quantized
IT 8 model. You would choose TF Light
when your training stack uses Tensor
Flow. And your deployment targets are
phones, IOT devices, or other edged
hardwares. In that world, TF Lite is
actually the most natural choice. Third,
Open Venow. Open Venow is a toolkit
from Intel for optimising and deploying
models on Intel hardwares, CPUs,
integrated GPUs, and Intel accelerators.
The typical flow is start from a model
in ONNX, or tensor flow, convert it
into open window intermediate
representation.
Then run it with open window runtime on
Intel machines.
This is especially attractive when your
infra is heavily Intel based. You want
a strong inference performance on CPUs
without needing GPUs.
So if your world is lot of Intel
servers, Open window is a strong option.
Let's put this into simple mental map.
ONNX is your general purpose cross
platform or cross framework option.
Great for cloud servers, CPU and GPU
deployments, especially when different
teams train models in different
frameworks. Tensorflow Light is your
mobile and edge option in the
Tensorflow ecosystem. Perfect for
Android, iOS and IOT deployments. Open
Veno is your Intel first option.
Ideal when your workload run mostly on
Intel CPUs and integrated GPUs. And you
want better CPU performance without
introducing GPUs. In a real company,
you might use more than one of these
different products can pick the best
fit for their constraints.
