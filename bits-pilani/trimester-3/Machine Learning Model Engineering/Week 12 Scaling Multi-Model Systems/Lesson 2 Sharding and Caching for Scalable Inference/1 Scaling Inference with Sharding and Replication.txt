Let's quickly recap the scaling problem.
In simplest system, you have one model
service. On one machine and all traffic
flows through that instance. This is
easy to understand, but as traffic
grows, you will see increased latency,
timeouts, and a huge single point of
failure.
There is also a hard upper bound on how
many queries per second that one
machine can handle.
To go beyond that, we use two ideas
replication, where we run multiple
copies of the same model to spread load,
and sharding, where we divide traffic
or data into pieces and send each piece
to a specific subset of servers or
models.
