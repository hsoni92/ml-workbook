Welcome to Module 12, Topic 3,
Multi-tenant Systems and resource
isolation. In a real machine learning
platform, it's rare to have just one
team or one model. Instead, you have
many tenants, different product teams,
internal business units, or even
external customers, all using the same
infrastructure. Our goal is to share
infrastructure efficiently without
letting one tenant hurt another. We
don't want performance interference,
data leaks, or broken SLOs. In this
video, we will build a mental model for
multi tenant system and the technique
we use to keep them safe and
predictable.
Let's define tenant. In machine
learning platform, a tenant is
basically a logical customer of the
platform, that might be an internal
product team, like search,
recommendations, or fraud, a business
unit, like retail, enterprise, or small
business.
Or an external customer. If you are
offering machine learning as a platform
or SAS product, each tenant usually has
its own models, data, SLOs, and budgets,
but they all run on the same sharded
hardware and infrastructure.
That's where the challenges comes in.
In a multi tenant setup, the platform
provides the heavy lifting, compute,
storage, networking, and common tools
for deployment, monitoring and logging.
Tenants bring their own models,
pipelines and traffic. Some tenants
generate steady traffic, others are
spiky, some are high priority, others
are best effort. There is a natural
tension here. You get efficiency by
sharing the resources, but you also
need safety and isolation so that one
tenant doesn't accidentally breaks
the experience for everyone else. One
of the classic multi-tenant issues is
the noisy neighbours problem. The noisy
neighbour is a tenant that overuses
shared resources, CPU, GPU, memory,
network, to the point that the other
tenant starts to suffer, latency
increases, queues build up, timeout
happens, and. Users for other teams get
worse experience. For example,
Tenant A might launch a big batch job
on shared cluster, and suddenly Tenant
B online conference service becomes
sluggish. The core challenge of
multi-tenancy is how do we let tenants
share the same resource. Efficiently,
but keep noisy neighbours under control.
