Let's start with batch ingestion. Batch
injection runs on a fixed schedule,
maybe once a day at 1:00 a.m., or every
hour for the past 5 hours.
Each job reads data, raw data, for a
specific time window, like yesterday's
events, Clean it, join it and other
table and computes aggregations. And
write the result into your new data
warehouse for feature Tables.
This pattern is simple and extremely
common in machine learning workflow.
Where does batch injection Sign in
Machine Learning? It's ideal for
regular retraining jobs like updating a
churn model every week with a new
labelled data, offline scoring, such as
computing monthly risks, scores, or
nightly recommendations for all users,
and heavy aggregations, for example, 30
or 90 days rolling statistics and
complex feature engineering. If your
use case doesn't need instant reaction,
batch is often the easiest and most
robust choice. Here is the concrete
batch example for training data. Once
per day, say at 10 a.m., we read
yesterday's event from the partitions
like events 2024, 2009, 2010.
We join them with reference table like
users or products, we compute features
and write a daily feature table.it
training_features_202409010. We append
that to a master training data set,
which our retraining pipeline can use.
This kind of job might take minutes or
even hours, and that's perfectly fine
