Now let's define our micro batching
ingestion.
Micro batch is still batching, but the
batches are smaller, and they run much
more frequently, like every 1 to 5
minutes. Conceptually, it feels like
almost streaming, but implemented as
many small batch jobs. You might
implement this with spark structured
streaming, Flink or beam in micro batch
mode.
Or even with a corn job that runs a
small script every few minutes.
Micro batch is great when you need
freshers feature.
You don't Need per event updates. For
example, updating user session features
every 2 minutes. Refreshing risk or
fraud scores every 5 minutes.
Recomputing last 15 minutes of clicks
periodically. It's a nice compromise.
Latency is much lower than a daily
batch, but the architecture and tooling
can remain closer to batch, which many
teams already understand.
Here is a micro batch example for risk
scoring.
Every 5 minutes a job, read the last 5
minutes of transactions. Compute
features such as spend in last 5 and 6
minutes. Or numbers of failed loggings.
Runs a risk model and writes updated
risk score values into database.
And downstream services or dashboard
read that latest risk score when they
need it. You get a near real time risk
score without building a fully event by
event streaming system. Thank you.
