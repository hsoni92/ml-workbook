Under the hood, most machine learning
pipelines still rely on classic ETL or
ELT.
In ETL, extract, transform, load, you
extract data from operational system,
logs or APIs. Transform it by cleaning,
joining and aggregating, then load the
result into data warehouse or data lake.
In ELT, extract, load, transform, you
load raw data into the warehouse first.
Then, do most transformations there,
using SQL or other tools.
For machine learning, ETL and ELT
pipelines produces the clean,
structured table that you then turn
into features for training and scoring.
