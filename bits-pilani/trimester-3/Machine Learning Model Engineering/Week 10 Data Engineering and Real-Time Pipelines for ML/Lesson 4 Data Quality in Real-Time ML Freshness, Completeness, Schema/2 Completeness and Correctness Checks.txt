Freshness is just one dimension of load
quality.
We also need to think about
completeness and correctness.
Things that can go wrong, missing
events when upstream system drop
message or pipeline fails, duplicates
due to retries or at least one delivery
semantic, out of order events where
data arrives late or with wrong time
stamp. And invalid values, like
negative amounts, not a number, ances,
or impossible categories.
These issues won't usually crash your
code, instead, they quietly make
features biassed or misleading, and
your model performance degrades. You
don't need a huge framework to start
monitoring data quality.
For each window, like 5 minutes or an
hour, you can compute simple count of
events overall and per key, and watch
for sudden drop or spikes. Track
distributions of important fields,
means standard deviations.
Min and max and category frequencies.
Measure the null or invalid value rate
for key fields.
Then you set up alerts if these metrics
deviate too far from historical
baseline.
This already catches many issues before
they silently poison your machine
learning pipeline.
