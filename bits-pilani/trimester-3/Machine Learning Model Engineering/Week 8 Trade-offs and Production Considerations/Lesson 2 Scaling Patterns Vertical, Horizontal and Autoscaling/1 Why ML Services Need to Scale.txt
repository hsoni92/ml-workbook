Welcome to scaling patterns, vertical,
horizontal and auto scaling. In the
previous video, we saw production
machine learning as a tug of war
between accuracy, latency, cost and
user experience. In this video, we will
focus on one big question.
When traffic goes up, how do we keep
latency and UX under control without
blowing up the cost? We will look at 3
classical patterns vertical scaling or
scaling up, horizontal scaling or
scaling out, and auto scaling, where
the system adjusts capacity
automatically based on load.
And we will tie each of these back to
latency, cost, and user experience,
first. Let us remind ourselves why
scaling matters. Real machine learning
APIs do not see a flat, predictable
trickle of traffic. They see spikes
during marketing campaign or big events,
daily or weekly pattern, and hopefully
steady growth as the product succeeds.
If we do nothing about just keep one
fixed instance, what happens? Lancies
start to rise as the instance gets
overloaded. Error rates can increase,
and our service level objectives, or
SLOs under P95 latency and availability
start getting violated.
Scaling is all about adding capacity in
smart way, so that we can keep latency
and error rates within target as load
changes.
There are two fundamental ways to add
capacity. The first is vertical scaling,
often called scaling up. You take your
existing machine and replace it with a
bigger one, more CPUs, more memory,
maybe a more powerful GPUs, graphical
processing unit. And second is
horizontal scaling or scaling out
instead of one bigger box. You run
multiple copies of your service, and
place a load balancer in the front of
distribution traffic. Because
approaches are trying to solve the same
problem, keep latency under control
when load grows. Increase the number of
requests per second, and system can
handle.
