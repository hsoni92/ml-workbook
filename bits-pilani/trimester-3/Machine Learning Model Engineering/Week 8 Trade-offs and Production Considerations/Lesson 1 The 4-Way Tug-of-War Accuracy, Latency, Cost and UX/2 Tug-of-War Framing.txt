I want you to picture a tug of war game.
In the centre, there is a knot on the
rope that represents your production
machine learning system. On one side,
accuracy is pulling, trying to make the
model bigger, deeper and more powerful.
On the other side, latency is pulling,
asking model to respond it in only a
few milliseconds.
On the third side, cost is pulling,
trying to keep hardware and cloud bills
under control. And finally, user
experience, or UX is pulling, wanting
everything to feel fast, reliable, and
intuitive. In practise, you're not
trying to win on just one side, you are
constantly adjusting the position of
the knot. To a place where is
acceptable or your product or and to
your company.
Let's ground this in a few quick
stories. First story. The team trains a
new, more complex model. Often metric
looks great, accuracy improves by 3%
points, but in production, response
time doubles, page load slows, user
abandons the flow, and overall business
metrics get worse. Second story. Your
team focuses on latency. They ship a
tiny model that is extremely fast, but
its predictions are poor. Customers see
bad recommendations, wrong flags, or
annoying false positives, and they lose
trust. Third story finance pushes for
cost reduction. The team cuts hardware
capacity aggressively. The system
survives, but now application is often
slow or jittery. User notices, reviews
get worse, and growth slowdowns. All
three stories are the same pattern.
optimising one dimension in isolation
hurts the others.
