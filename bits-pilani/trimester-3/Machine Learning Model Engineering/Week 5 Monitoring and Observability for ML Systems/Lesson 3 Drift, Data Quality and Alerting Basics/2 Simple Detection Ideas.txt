How do we actually measure drift?
Start simple per feature, track means
and standard deviations over time. Look
at min max and missing rates, plot
histograms comparing training data to a
recent time window. On top of that,
there are classic drift tests.
PSI Population Stability Index as a
high level stability score.
KS test
Kalagorov-imirov for continuous
variables.
She square test, or frequency
comparison for categorical features.
The basic idea is always the same. Take
a reference distribution.
Maybe training data or a stable period.
Compare it It with the recent video,
last week or last month, and flag
features, those distributions have
shifted beyond some threshold. You
don't need perfect statistics. You need
useful, explainable signals.
Once you can measure drift, the next
question is, when do we actually alert
human?
Good candidates for alerts. Several key
features show strong drift at the same
time. Label rates or overall moral
performance cross a bad threshold. A
critical segment, say a country or
product line, show a big change. But we
also want to avoid alert fatigue. Don't
page load for every tiny bump?
Look for sustained or significant drift.
Group related signals into a single dri
drift incident, instead of spamming
slack.
Route alert to the right owners. Some
issues are data pipeline problems, some
are model issues, some are infra issues.
The goal is that an alert means stop
and look at this, something materially
changed, not mute this channel because
it's always noisy. Thank you
