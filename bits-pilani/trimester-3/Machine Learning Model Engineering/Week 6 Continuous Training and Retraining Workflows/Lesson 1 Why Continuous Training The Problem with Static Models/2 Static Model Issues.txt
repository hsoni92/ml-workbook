Let's situate retraining. In the life
cycle we have used throughout the
course. After we train and deploy a
model, we monitor it in production.
Over time, the world changes, data
shifts and performance drifts.
Retraining is not version 2.0 once and
done, it's a loop.
Deploy, monitor, detect meaningful
change, retrain with new data, and
deploy again. The question is, what
counts as a meaningful change that's
worth spinning up that loop?
