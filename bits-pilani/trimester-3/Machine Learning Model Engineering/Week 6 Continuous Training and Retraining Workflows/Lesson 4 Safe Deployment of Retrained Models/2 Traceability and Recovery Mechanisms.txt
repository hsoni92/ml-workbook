Governance also means having a solid
audit trail.
For every production model version, you
want a registry entry with its version,
owner, and current stage.
Training lineage.
Which data snapshot, code commit, and
config were used? And deployment
history.
When it moved to staging and production,
and who approved it, this makes root
cause analysis far easier. If
performance drops, you can see exactly
what changed and when. It actually
supports external audits and compliance
requirements, where you may need to
show which data and model was it
trained on, how it behaved over time,
and who was responsible for each change.
Even with careful evaluation. You
should assume something will eventually
go wrong. That's why you need a
rollback plan. Keep the previous
version. Or the champion model
available, not overwritten or deleted.
Use version pinning in config, for
example, like model version 7 instead
of saying it latest.
Make roll rolling back as simple as
changing that version or flipping a
flag.
And don't just have a rollback
procedure on paper, test it.
As part of your deployment playbook,
check that you can actually route
traffic back to the last good model. A
fast, reliable rollback turns an
incident from a crisis into a
manageable event.
