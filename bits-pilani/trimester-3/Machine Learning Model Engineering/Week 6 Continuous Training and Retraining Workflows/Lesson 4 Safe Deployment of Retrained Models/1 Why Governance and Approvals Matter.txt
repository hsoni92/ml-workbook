Why do we need governance at all?
Without it, you end up with. Silent
model changes in production, no clear
owner when something breaks, and
painful questions like who approved
this, and which model live last Tuesday.
Governance is not about showing
everything down for fun, it's about
accountability, knowing who owns and
approves the changes, traceability.
Being able to reconstruct what happened
and why, and controlled risk, making
sure big changes get proper review,
especially in regulated or high impact
domains. This is nonnegotiable. The
first governance pillar is approval and
change management. For each important
model, define a model owner or owning
team. And specific honours for major
architectural changes, promotions to
productions. And where relevant
fairness or ethics reviews. In practise,
approvals often happen through pull
requests that includes a summary of a
new model and its metrics, change
tickets that link to model registry
entries, experiment results, and AB
test outcomes. The key is that
promoting a model is not an invisible
push to main, it's a visible,
reviewable change with clear yes from
right people.
