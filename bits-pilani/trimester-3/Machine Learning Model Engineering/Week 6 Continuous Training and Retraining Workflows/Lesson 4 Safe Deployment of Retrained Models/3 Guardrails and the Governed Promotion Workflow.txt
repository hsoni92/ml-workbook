Governance also include guard rails,
hard limits and safety checks around
the model. Some common ones are rate
limiting to prevent a spike in traffic
for overwhelming your model or
downstream systems. Output sanity
checks ensure probability stays in,
let's say 01, scores stay in expected
range, and no obviously impossible
values are returned.
Kill switches, feature flags or config
switches that let you turn off a risky
model or a feature quickly. Policy
constraint, technical enforcement that
certain features or attributes are not
used in the model, spatially sensitive
ones. The mindset is
Don't rely on model always behaving.
Add rails so that when doesn't, you
still stay within safe bound.
Let's zoom out and see the full
governed life cycle in one picture.
We monitor our production model and
detect drift. Performance changes or
policy triggers. We run a retrain
pipeline to produce candidate models
and evaluate them. A governance layer
ensures proper approval, model
registration and lineage tracking and
auditability.
We do a controlled rollout, staging,
optional shadow, or AB, then full
production. Throughout, we rely on
guard rails and rollback mechanism to
contain risk. This is what it looks
like to move from we trained a model to
we operate a model safely and
responsibly over time. Thank you.
