Before you hit the retrand button.
Run through a checklist, quick
checklist. Is there a real persistent
change in data, performance or policy?
Could we fix this by tweaking threshold,
adding a rule, or fixing a data bug
instead of retraining? Do we have
enough fresh data and labels to
actually learn something better? And do
we have a pipeline to train, evaluate
and compare against the current model
in control way? If these answers are
yes.
Then retraining, triggering retraining
is sensible.
In the next video we'll talk about
What that retraining and promotion
pipelines actually looks like. Thank
you.
