Next step toward live evaluation is
shadow testing, sometimes called as
dark launch. In this setup,
Light traffic comes in as usual. Both
the current champion and the new
challenger model sees the same input,
but only the champion output is used to
respond the user. We log the
challenger's predictions and analyse
them offline.
Do they look sane?
How do metric compares?
Do we see any obvious bias or failures?
The benefits
We are using real production inputs.
But we are not putting users at risk.
The tradeoffs
We pay extra compute.
We generate more logs.
And we need to be careful with privacy
and data retention.
Shadow testing is an excellent way to
de-risk a new model before any user see
it.
Once you're comfortable with offline
and shadow results. You may choose to
run an A B test. In an AB test, some
fraction of users or requests go to the
champion. Another fraction goes to the
challenger, and you measure real
business outcomes, revenue, click,
fraud loss, churn, etc. all of these.
This tells you not just whether the new
model looks better. But whether it
actually improves behaviour on business
metrics in real world.
You do need enough traffic and. Time to
reach statistical significance. A clear,
defined primary matrix and guard rails.
Also, be careful not to run too many
overlapping experiments.
That interfere with each other. AB
tests are the gold standard, but they
are heavier weight, so you don't use
them for every tiny change.
