Let's make it concrete. Why do
multimodal system appears?
One big reason is localization and
segmentation. You may have different
models for different countries,
languages, or customer tiers.
Another is versioning and
experimentations. You might run an old
version and a new version side by side
for canary deployment, AB tests, or
champion Challenger setup.
You also split by task. One model
handles fraud scores, another handles
credit risk, another predictions,
another drives recommendations, and
finally, architecture and scale. You
might have model per product line, per
tenant, or per use case. The result is,
a typical production platform has many
models, not one. So routing and
ensembles become core patterns. At a
high level, there are two main patterns.
Routing means choosing one model to
handle each request. A router. Often
simple business logic decides which
model to call. Assembles means calling
multiple models on the same input and
then combining their output. For
example, By averaging scores or taking
a majority vote, both patterns try to
match the request with the right
expertise, and often to secure accuracy
and robustness compared to single model.
