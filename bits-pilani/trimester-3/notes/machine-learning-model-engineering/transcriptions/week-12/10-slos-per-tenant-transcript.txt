To manage expectations in multi tenant
system, we use SLOs, Service Level
Objectives.
An SLO is a target for metric like
latency, availability, or error rate.
Not every tenant has the same SLOs. A
VIP external customer might get
tight latency target and very high
availability, while an internal, low
priority tenant might get more relaxed
best effort guarantees. For example,
tenant A, 95th percentile latency under
150 milliseconds and 99.9% availability.
Tenant B, 95th percentile latency,
under 400 milliseconds, and 99%
availability. These SLOs drive capacity
planning, isolation strategies, and
incident response. They tell you who
gets protected first when things go bad.
To handle multi-tenancies safely, we
use isolation strategies at several
layers logical isolation, separate name
space, projects, or accounts so each
tenant has its own scope, resource
isolation, quotas and limits on CPU,
memory, GPUs, and concurrency, so one
tenant can't consume everything. Data
and access isolation, separate data
stores, odd schemas, and strict access
controls so tenants can't see each
other's data. The general idea is
shared platform but clear boundaries.
Tenants feel like they are in their own
environment, even though they share the
underlying hardware.
