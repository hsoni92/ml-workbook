Or Downstream Predictor.
In this session, we will stay high
level, the goal is clear mental model
of embeddings, vector databases and
rack pipeline, not a deep dive into
specific tool or configs. First,
embedding. An embedding is just a
numeric vector that represents
something like a piece of text, an
image, an item, or a user, in a way
that captures meaning. The key property
is similar items end up with nearby
vectors, and dissimilar items are far
apart in this vector space. These
embeddings are learned by model, so
that geometry reflects semantics. Two
sentences with similar meaning have
embeddings that are close together,
even if they don't share many words.
Once we have embeddings. We can do
similarity search. The pattern is, take
your query or item, compute its
embeddings, and then find the nearest
neighbours in the existing set of
vectors. We typically measure
similarity with cosine similarity, dot
product, or sometimes equilidean
distance. This is the core semantic
search, more like this, recommendations,
near duplicate detections, and many
cluster workflows vector search answers
the questions. Given the things, what
else looks similar in embedded space?
