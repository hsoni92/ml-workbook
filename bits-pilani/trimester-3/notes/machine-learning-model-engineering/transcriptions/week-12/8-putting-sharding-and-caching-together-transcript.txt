Let's put everything together.
In a practical, large scale setup, a
router decides which shard or model to
hit, maybe by region, language, or
tenets. Within each shard, replicas of
the model handle the load for
reliability and throughput.
A cache sits in the front of model
calls, storing outputs or embeddings
with version keys, so repeated or
similar request can be served quickly.
This combination of routing, sharing,
replication and caching lets you keep
latency low, throughput high, and SLO
predictable, as traffic and the number
of model grow.
