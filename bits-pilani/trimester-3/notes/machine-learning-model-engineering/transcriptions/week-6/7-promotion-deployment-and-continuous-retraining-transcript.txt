Stage 5 is about getting a new model
safely into production.
A common pattern.
Deploy to a staging environment first.
And then run smoke test or replay
recorded traffic.
Then run a canary or shadow deployment
in production. Send a small percentage
of live traffic into the new model, or
run it in parallel and lock predictions
without affecting users. If metric
looks good, promote it to the full
production. But keep the model, old
model around so that you can roll back.
During this phase, monitoring is
crucial. You're not just checking that
the model runs, but that it behaves as
expected. Under the real traffic
compared to the previous champion.
Putting it all together, retraining
becomes a closed loop. Monitoring feeds
us signals, repeated drift. On
important features. Performance or SLO
breaches?
Concerning changes in business KPIs.
Those signals trigger a retraining
workflow, sometimes automatically,
sometimes via ticket that human
approves. The retraining pipeline.
Snapshot data, train candidates,
compare them to the champion, register
the winner, and promote it through
staging and canary to production.
Human judgement still matters,
especially around risk and fairness.
But the pipeline gives you repeatable.
Auditable path from we saw drift to we
deployed a better model. Thank you.
