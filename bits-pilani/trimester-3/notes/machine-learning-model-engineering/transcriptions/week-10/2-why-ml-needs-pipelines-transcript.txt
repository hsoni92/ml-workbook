Let's contrast the world.
Of a notebook with the world of
production pipeline. In a notebook, you
manually load a CSV or run one of SQQL
query, do some ad hoc cleaning and join.
Runs, sells, on whatever order works,
and as long as it works, once you're
happy in production, that's not enough.
Data needs to show up every day, every
hour, or even every second. Steps must
be automated, repeatable and observable,
with clear alerts when something breaks.
This jumps from one of notebook to
reliable pipeline is one of the biggest
shift when turning machine learning
into a real product.
So where do data pipeline fits in the
machine learning cycle?
They feed at least 3 major stages.
Training, where we use historical data
to build model, validation, where we
evaluate models on fresh or held out
data, and inference, where we compute
features and predictions in production.
If data is late, training and batch
scoring gets delayed. If data is wrong,
your validation metrics lie and your
production decision break. That's why
just run this notebook again is not a
strategy, you need robust data flow.
