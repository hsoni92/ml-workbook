Where does streaming machine learning

detection, spotting unusual traffic
patterns, suspicious transactions, or
system failures?
Live recommendations and
personalization, where you want to
react quickly to recent clicks and
views, dynamic pricing or bidding,
reacting to market data and demand. And
even observability, where you stream
model prediction and metrics into a
monitoring system for real time
alerting.
Streaming machine learning often pairs
with. Online inference, where an API is
called per request, or streaming
inference where a job consumes events
and emits prediction or alerts directly
on the stream. How do we choose between
micro batch and full streaming?
Micro batch is easier, if you already
use batch tools, it's usually enough
when you just need minute level
freshness.
Streaming is worth the complexity when
you need 2nd level or sub 2nd level
reactions. Every high event volume or
reach continuous features.
A good rule of thumb, start with a
batch or micro batch.
Move to full streaming only when you
have clear requirement for very low
latency. Or when simpler patterns can't
keep up.
Streaming is not just for features. It
can touch multiple parts. Of the
machine learning life cycle, it can
power features computation, maintaining
live aggregates and latest stages that
feeds into online feature stores. It
can drive model serving. Where a
streaming job, consume events, runs
models and writes prediction to other
topic or store. It can support
monitoring by streaming predictions,
feature risks and eventual labels, so
streaming can become a backbone of data
for data.
