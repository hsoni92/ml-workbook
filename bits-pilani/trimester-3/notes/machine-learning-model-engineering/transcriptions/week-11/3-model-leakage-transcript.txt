Third bucket, model and privacy threats.
With model extraction, someone sends a
large number of queries to your public
API and uses the input and output to
train your own copy or approximation of
your model. That can expose your
intellectual property and make it
easier for attacker to study weaknesses
offline.
There is also information leakage. If
your model is overfitted or your output
are too detailed, predictions may
reveal patterns about specific
individuals or rare events in the
training data.
In the worst case, this can become a
privacy or compliance issue. Finally,
over sharing internals. Like
downloadable downloadable weights.
Verbs, error messages, or forgotten
debug end points. Make all of this
easier for an attacker.
The principle, expose only what's
needed, and treat model as sensitive
asset, just like source code or
databases.
