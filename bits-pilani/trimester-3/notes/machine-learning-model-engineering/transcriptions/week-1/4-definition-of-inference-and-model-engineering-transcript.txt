Let's put a clear definition on this.
Machine learning model engineering is
everything needed to make models usable
by other system and team reliable under
real world condition. And scalable as
traffic grows or grows down.
Practically that means taking a trained
model artefact, maybe. PKL or a PT file.
A saved checkpoint.
And turning it into a production grade
service that's integrated, monitored,
can be trusted by the rest of the
organisation. When you hear model
engineering in this course, think we're
not talking about inventing the model
ideas, we're talking about making it
work in the real world. It's helpful to
separate data science or research from
model engineering.
Even though in a small team, the same
person might do both. On the data
science research side, the focus is on
exploring data, generating model,
building prototypes in notebook,
compare different approaches with
experiments and metrics. However, on
the model engineering side, the focus
shifts to turning those prototypes into
robust services, owning deployment,
scaling, monitoring, making sure the
model is integrated into real
applications and workflow. You can
think of it this way. Data science
answers, what model should we use?
Model engineering answers, how do we
run this model safely in production?
Every day.
