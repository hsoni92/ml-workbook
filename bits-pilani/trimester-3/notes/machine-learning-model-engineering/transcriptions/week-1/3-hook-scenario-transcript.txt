Let's start with a story that might
feel familiar. Imagine you're on your
team, have trained a model in a
notebook, it gets great metric, maybe
your accuracy is fantastic, your error
is much lower than before, everyone is
excited, and then nothing happens. The
model never gets integrated into the
product. No customers ever see it. A
few months later, the notebook is
basically forgotten.
This is extremely common.
The obvious question is why.
Keep that question in mind. Because the
answer is what model engineering is all
about. So what's actually sitting
between the notebook and real user? A
real product never gives or it's more
than just a train model.
It needs API, or services, so other
systems can call it. It needs
infrastructure to run reliably and at a
scale. It needs monitoring so someone
notices when things breaks or drift. If
those pieces are not there. The model
never leaves the lab, it's technically
done in the notebook, but practically
useless to the business. That missing
layer in the middle, the thing that
connects we have a model.
To this is a reliable product feature,
is exactly the space where machine
learning model engineering lives.
