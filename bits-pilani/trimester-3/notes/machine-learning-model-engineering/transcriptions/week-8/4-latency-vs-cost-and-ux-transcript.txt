Now let us zoom in to the triangle of
latency, cost, and user experience. If
you want to reduce latency, you can add
more replicas of your service. Or move
to bigger machine or GPUs.
That usually improves UX, but it also
increases your infrastructure cost. If
you want to reduce costs, you can
shrink instances or reduce the number
of replicas, but if you go too far,
latency starts to climb and the system
becomes unstable under the load, and UX
gets worse.
In later video in this module, we will
talk about scaling pattern and cost
optimisation techniques that helps you
balance this triangle more
intelligently. A practical way to use
this mental model is to turn it into a
checklist. For any change you make,
introducing a new model, changing
configuration, or changing
infrastructure, ask these four
questions. Accuracy. Did our offline
online quality metrics go up, down, or
stay the same? How? By how much?
Latency.
What happened to both average latency
and P95 latency? Cost. What does this
do to cost per request or to our
monthly bills? User experience.
If you were the user, does the change
feel snappier, more helpful, or more
frustrating? If you cannot answer these
ones. That is a sign of you might be
missing an important effect.
