Despite their differences, ranking and
fraud system shares a lot. Both need
reliable data pipeline from raw events
to usable tables. Both use a feature
layer or feature store to keep training
and serving consistent. Both rely on
online scoring service that perform low
latency inference. Both requires
continuous monitoring and retraining.
Metric drift, data drift, and model
performance all need to be tracked over
time. In other words, they run on top
of the same machine learning platform
primitives you have been studying
throughout this course.
The big difference are that they
optimise for and the cost of getting it
wrong. Ranking system focus on order
and relevance. They optimise click
through conversion and engagement.
They experiment aggressively with AB
tests. A bad result is annoying and may
hurt metric, but it's usually not
catastrophic. Fraud systems focus on
risk and correctness. They care about
fraud loss and group specific error
rates. Decisions are often one shot,
with strong compliance and audit
requirements. A bad decision mean can
mean direct financial losses or unfair
treatment of users.
The toolbox, data pipeline feature
stores, model serving, monitoring is
similar. But the priorities and risk
profiles are very different.
Understanding that distinction is
crucial for designing the right
architecture. Thank you.
