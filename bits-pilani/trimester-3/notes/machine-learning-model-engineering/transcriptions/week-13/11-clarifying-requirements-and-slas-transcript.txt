Welcome to Module 13, Topic 4, Systems
design interview lens, SLAs, traffic,
and failure scenarios. In this video,
we will shift from implementation
details to how you reason about machine
learning systems overall, especially in
a system design interview or an
architecture review.
We will ask questions like.
What are we building and who depends on
it?
How much traffic and data will it see?
What happens when we when pieces break?
The idea is to use everything from
Module 1 to Module 12 as a toolbox and
apply it in a structured interview
style way.
In any system design conversation,
don't start by drawing boxes, start by
clarifying requirements. What exactly
is the product? Is this a
recommendation system, a fraud detector,
or a search rankings service? Who uses
it and how? Is this a UI facing API, a
back end service, or a purely batch
system? What does good enough mean? Is
the priority is accuracy, freshness, or
user experience, and, crucially, what
happens if it fails?
Is it just a slightly worse homepage,
or is it a regulatory or a financial
incident?
These answers will shape your choice
for SLA's scaling and resilience. Thank
you.
