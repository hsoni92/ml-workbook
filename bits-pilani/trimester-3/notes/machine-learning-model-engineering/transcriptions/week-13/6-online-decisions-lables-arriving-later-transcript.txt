Now let's switch to fraud detection.
Think of an online payment. The user
presses pay. And the system must decide.
Approve, decline, or step up with extra
verification, like one time password.
The latency budget is extremely tight,
often just a few tens of milliseconds,
and errors are expensive. A false
negative means you approve a fraudulent
transaction and lose money. A false
positive mean you block a legitimate
customer, which hurts revenue and user
trust.
These are often one shot decision, with
limited evidence and strong compliance
implication, the architecture reflect
that. The online flow for fraud
detection looks like this. A
transaction event comes into in with
card details, amount, merchant, device,
and location. We quickly assemble real
time features, such as how many
transactions this card has made in the
last 10 minutes.
Whether the device is new or known.
Whether the IP addresses look
suspicious or unusual.
We feed these into a fraud model.
Which output a risk score between 0 and
1. A decision engine applies threshold.
Low risk, approve, very high risk,
decline, middle reason, step up
verification like an OTP.
We log the decision and context for
future audits and training. Everything
must be extremely fast and robust
because this sits directly in the
payment flow.
Fraud systems also have complex offline
side.
Labels for fraud often arrive late, for
example, when a customer files a
chargeback or an investigation confirms
fraudulent activity days or weeks after
the transaction. We then mark past
transactions as fraud or not fraud,
reconstruct the features that were
available at decision time, and build
training data set from that history.
New models are trained on these
labelled data set with evaluation
focused on the cost of error, not just
overall accuracy. We tune thresholds
differently by region, merchant type,
or risk appetite.
Some segment may tolerate a higher
false positive rate to reduce fraud
loss, and others might prioritise user
experience. This creates a feedback
loop similar to ranking but with very
different stakes.
