Even with a clean architecture,
recommendation systems are hard to run
in practise.
First, there is a freshness. User
behaviour shifts quickly. What a user
cared about yesterday might not matter
today. So your feature, and sometimes
your model, must keep up. Second, You
are balancing many objectives, pure
relevance, diversity, business rules
like promotions or inventory, and long
term satisfaction. You can easily
overoptimize for short term clicks and
hurt long term trust. Third, the model
stack is operationally complex. You
must stay within strict latency budget,
handle traffic spikes, and keep the
data and feature pipeline healthy.
That's why monitoring is essential.
Online metrics like click through and
conversational conversation rates,
model matrics and breakdowns across
user segments, system metrics like
latency and error rates. These signals
feed into retraining, experimentations
and system design decisions.
Exactly the themes you have seen
throughout the module 11 and 12. Thank
you.
