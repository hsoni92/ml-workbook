Hi everyone. Welcome back to this
module. In this 3rd week, we are
shifting our focus on how marketers
actually use information to make
smarter decisions. We will explore
marketing information ecosystem, or
also known as MIE, along with market
research approaches and basic
statistics that support real world
marketing choices. And through hands-on
examples you will see how modern tools
helps marketeers estimate demand, track
trends, and understand markets better.
By the end of this module, you will be
able to achieve 3 important learning
outcomes that are essential for modern
marketing practise. First, you will be
able to define market research and
clearly distinguish between the
different types. You will understand
what market research really means in a
business context and why organisations
rely on it before making major
decisions. More importantly, you will
be able to differentiate between
primary research, where companies
collect original data directly from
consumers, and secondary research where
existing external database is leveraged.
This will help you recognise when fresh
customised insights are required and
when existing information is sufficient
to guide decisions.
Second, you will be able to compare
different statistical approaches used
in market research. Marketing decisions
often include trade-offs between speed
and accuracy, cost and depth, or scale
and precision. In this model, you will
learn how statistics help marketeers
summarise data, compare alternatives,
and evaluate uncertainty. You will also
understand why certain statistical
models are chosen over the others and
how these choices influence the quality
and the reliability of marketing
insights. This will allow you to better
interpret research findings, rather
than simply accepting numbers at face
value. Third, you will also implement
secondary research and basic demand
estimation using modern marketing
technology tools. You will see how
marketeers use digital platforms to
study market trends, analyse
competition, and estimate demand before
launching products or campaigns. These
tools make it possible to quickly
explore markets, validate ideas, and
support decisions with data without
always. Conducting expensive or time
consuming primary research. By the end
of this module, you will not just
understand what market research is, you
will also understand how marketers
think about information, how they
combine data from multiple sources and
how they turn this data into actionable
insights. Most importantly, you will
see how these insights directly shape
real businesses' decision making in the
marketing process.
