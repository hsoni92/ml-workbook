Let's start by understanding the growth
share metrics, which is a portfolio
management framework that helps
companies decide how to prioritise
their different businesses based on
their degree of profitability. It's
kind of created by the famous
consulting company called BCG and is
known as BCG Growth Share Metrics. The
matrix is divided into 4 quadrants, and
each quadrant represents a specific
combination of relative market share
and market growth. Products with lows
with low growth and high market share
are referred to as cash cows. Companies
should milk these cash cows for cash
that can be reinvested elsewhere. Those
with high growth and high market share
are known as starts. Companies should
significantly invest in these products
as they have future potential in terms
of returns. When a product shows high
growth but low market share, it falls
into the question mark category.
Companies should decide whether to
invest further in these products or
discard them for now, depending on the
chances of becoming stars or cash cows.
Finally, products with low market share
and low growth are classified as pets.
Companies should consider liquidating,
divesting, or repositioning these
products. Together, all these 4
categories help a brand, company, or an
organisation take a structured approach
to managing their multiple product
portfolios and helping them make an
informed decision.
