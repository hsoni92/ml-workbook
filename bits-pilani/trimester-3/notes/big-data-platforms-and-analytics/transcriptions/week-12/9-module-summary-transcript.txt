Congratulations on completing Module 12,

in Tensorflow. I know it can be a lot
to take. Over the course of this module,
we have journeyed from high-level
architectural theories to the specific
APIs that make scaling possible. Let's
take a final look at the key concepts
that we have covered. We started by
comparing decentralised and centralised
learning. We saw that while parameter
servers are excellent for massive
sparse models like recommendation
engines, the ringing all reduce
strategy provides a bandwidth optimal
solution for that dense model, which is
common in modern deep learning.
We analysed the synchronisation
trade-offs. You now understand that
synchronous training offers
mathematical consistency at the cost of
the straggler problem, while
asynchronous training offers raw speed,
but can be trickier to converge due to
stale gradients.
And we have explored the practical side
with tensor flow strategies. We covered
mirrored strategy for maximising the
power of single workstation. And
multi-worker mirrored strategy for
scaling out a massive network cluster
using the Tensorflow config environment
variable. Finally, we peeked under the
hood at graphs and device placement,
learning how Tensorflow partitions
operations and users send and receive
nodes to orchestrate communication
across disparate hardware units.
Scaling intelligence across nodes is no
longer just an extra feature. It's a
requirement for modern AI development.
With the tools and concepts from this
module, you're now equipped to take
your models from a single machine to a
