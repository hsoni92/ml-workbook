SARC. Before we wrap up, let's take a
moment to synthesise everything we have
learned about Spark execution model. We
began by exploring the philosophy of
lazy evaluation. We learned that Spark
doesn't just execute line by line, it
waits for an action to be called, so it
can optimise the entire pipeline. This
laziness is what enables high-level
optimisation like predicate pushdown
and pipelining. Transforming your logic
into the most efficient physical plan
possible, we also dissected the anatomy
of a spark job. We saw how the driver
acts as the brain, building the lineage
and the dag. We learned that wide
dependencies, those operations that
require a shuffle, are the most
critical performance boundaries because
they force spark to break the job into
different stages by understanding the
difference between narrow
transformations. Stay local and wide
transformations which move data across
the network. You are now equipped to
identify potential bottlenecks in your
own big data application. The most
important key takeaway from this module
is that Spark is not just a tool for
processing data. It is an intelligent
execution engine. Every time you call
map or philtre or join, you aren't just
manipulating data, you are building a
complex optimised. Craft that Spark
will eventually execute in parallel
across your cluster. As we close this
module, I highly recommend that you

Definitive guide for a more granular
