Welcome to Lesson 2. In our last lesson,
we discussed why resilience is such a
high stakes requirement for big data.
This brings us to the specific
technical challenges of recovering data
in a distributed memory environment.
First, there is volatility and state
loss. Because Ram state is wiped.
Instantly upon the failure, if we don't
have a metadata-based recipe of our
data, we have no way of knowing where a
computation stopped or how to resume it.
You might ask, why not just copy the
data like STFS does? Well, that leads
to the replication bandwidth tax.
Traditional recovery via replication
requires copying data across the
network. If you're working with
terabytes of data in RAM, copying those
replicas consume massive amount of
bandwidth. And significantly slows down
your job.
Finally, we face consistency
bottlenecks. Trying to keep memory
replicas perfectly synced across a
global cluster introduces heavy latency
and complex locking issues.
This is why Spark's approach of using
lineage instead of replication is so
revolutionary.
It allows us to be 100 times faster
than Hadoop.
While remaining completely fault
tolerant.

where we'll deep dive into these
concepts. Till then, happy learning and
thank you so much.
