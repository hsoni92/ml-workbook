Welcome back. In our previous lessons,

walls that traditional computers
eventually hit. Today we are going to
look at the first strategy most
businesses use when they hit those
walls, and that is vertical scaling,
also known as scaling up. Think of
vertical scaling like this.
Imagine you are a delivery driver with
a small scooter. As your business grows,
you have one more package than the
scooter can carry your natural instinct
buy a bigger scooter, then a van, then
a massive truck. You are still just one
driver with one vehicle, but that
vehicle is becoming so powerful in the
world of servers. This means taking
your existing machine and swapping out
the parts for more RAM, a faster CPU,
or a larger hard drive. Why is this
great?
Number one, it is easy. You don't have
to change a single line of your code.
The software still thinks it's running
on one computer. That computer is just
faster.
2, low maintenance. You have 1 box to
manage, 1 operating system to update,
and one set of security rules.
So if vertical scaling is so easy, why
don't we use it for everything, right?
This is where we hit the industry
bottleneck, as discussed in the earlier
lessons. In big data, we often deal
with petabytes of data. There is no
truck in the world big enough to hold a
whole mountain, right? Eventually you
hit the three major problems. Number
one, the price wall. Moving from 16 GB
to 32 GB of RAM might cost you an extra
$20 a month, but moving from 1 TB to 2
TB or specialised high performance RAM
might cost you $20,000. The cost
doesn't go up in a straight line, it
explodes.
Then the hardware wall, you eventually
reach the physical limit of the
motherboard. You literally cannot plug
in more RAM or more CPUs because there
is no more slots.
And then the risk wall. This is a
single point of failure. If your one
super server has a power supply failure,
your entire multi-million dollar
business goes dark. One driver, one
truck. If the truck breaks, the
delivery stops. So what's the solution?
Industry experts use vertical scaling
as a short term fix. Or for specialised
tasks, if you're a small business where
high availability isn't a life or death
matter. Or if your data set fits
comfortably in memory, scaling up is
your best friend, because it keeps your
software simple, but if you are a
company like Netflix or Uber or Google,
where data never stops growing, you
eventually have to stop building a
bigger truck. And start building a
fleet of vans. In our next video, we'll
explore that fleet-based approach,
which is horizontal scaling. But for
now, remember, vertical scaling is
about making the individual stronger,
while big data is eventually about
making the group work together. See you
