We introduce the core architecture of
Storm, unlike Hadoop, which runs jobs
that eventually finish. Storm runs
topologies. A topology is a permanent
network of the processing logic that
runs until you manually kill it. This
makes it ideal for the unbounded data
that we have been discussing. There are
3 key architectural concepts on this
particular slide that you must
understand. Spouts and bolts, these are
the building blocks of any storm
application. Spouts are the sources of
the stream. Usually a spout will read
directly from the Kafka topics we
discussed in the previous video and
emit them into the topology. Bolts are
where the actual processing happens. A
bolt can philtre data, aggregate it,
join it with a database, or even talk
to a machine learning model. Your
distributed dag. Storm organises these
spouts and bolts into a directed
acyclic graph or dag. This graph
defines exactly how data flows from the
point of ingestion, which is the spout,
through various stages of
transformations, which are the bolts,
to the final output, high throughput,
and low latency. So this is the primary
reason why companies choose storm. It
is designed to process millions of
pupils per second. Across a cluster
with subsecond response times, often in
the range of milliseconds, this ultra
low latency is what makes storm a
preferred choice for high frequency
trading or real-time network monitoring.
