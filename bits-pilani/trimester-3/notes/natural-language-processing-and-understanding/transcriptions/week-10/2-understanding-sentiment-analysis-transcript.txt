Welcome back learners to this lesson on
understanding sentiment analysis.
Sentiment analysis is a specific type
of text classification where the goal
is to determine the emotional tone
behind the text.
The goal is to classify the sentiment
as positive, negative, or neutral.
Now check out this product review. I
absolutely love the screen resolution,
but the battery life is terrible. This
is an exercise for you. Pause the video
and tell me if the sentiment should be
positive, negative, or neutral for this
product review.
There are different levels of sentiment
analysis.
The first being document level, for
example, classifying the entire review.
Whether the movie entirely was positive,
negative, or neutral.
The second sentence level. For example,
classifying each product review
individually for the same product.
The 3rd, ABSA, aspect-based sentiment
analysis. This is the most advanced
level of sentiment analysis. It
identifies what the sentiment is about.
For example, consider this review. The
food was great, but the service was
slow.
Here, there are two sentiments. One is
positive and the second is negative.
The task is to identify the positive
was for which part and the negative was
for which part. In this example,
The food was positive, but the service
was negative.
Now sentiment analysis has its own
challenges.
Because computers struggle with human
nuances like sarcasm. Consider this
sentence. Oh great! My flight is
delayed again. This is a sarcastic
sentence. The words are positive, but
the meaning is negative.
Then negation.
The movie was not bad. Here not and bad,
both are negative. Double negative is a
positive.
The next challenge being the context.
The plot was unpredictable, for a
thriller movie, this is a positive
sentiment, whereas for a car driving
manual, this is a negative sentiment.
Then polysemy.
Consider these two sentences, the
battery life is long. Versus the wait
time was long.
For the context of battery life, Long
has a good sentiment.
Whereas for wait time, Long has a bad
sentiment. And that's all for this
video, and I'll see you in the next one.
Thank you.
