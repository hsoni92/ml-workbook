Welcome back, learners. In this lesson,
let's understand glove, another very
popular word embedding technique. Glove
stands for global vectors for word
representation. While word2vec relies
on local window context, glove relies
on global matrix factorization. It
builds a gigantic co-occurrence matrix
that counts how often every pair of
words appear together. In the entire
corpus, it then factorises this matrix
to produce the word vectors. Basically,
glove adds another layer over what word
to vec did with word frequencies and
their appearance. The intuition to
understand this is that the ratio of
probabilities of word cooccurrences
encodes the word meanings. For example,
if in a corpus, ice. And steam appear
rarely, but ice and solid appear often
together. This ratio captures the
concepts of thermodynamics. That's all
for this video. Thank you.
