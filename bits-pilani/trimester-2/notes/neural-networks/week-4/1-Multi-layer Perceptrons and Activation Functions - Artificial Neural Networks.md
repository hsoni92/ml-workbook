## Where We Are in the Course

- **Previous module:** Perceptron and logistic neuron; single neurons as linear classifiers; **XOR limitation**.
- That limitation showed that **single-layer models** are not expressive enough for many important problems.
- **This module:** Combine neurons into **multilayer networks** — the **multilayer perceptron (MLP)** and **activation functions**.

---

## What We Study This Week

- **MLP:** Simplest form of a neural network with hidden layers.
- **Hidden layers:** How they transform data representations; how information flows **forward** through an MLP.
- **Depth and width:** How they control **expressive power**.
- **Activation functions:** The source of all **non-linearity** in neural networks.

---

## Why MLPs Matter

- MLPs are not just one specific model — they are the **conceptual foundation** of deep learning.
- Almost every modern architecture (convolutional networks, recurrent networks, transformers) is built using the same core ideas: **layered computation** and **non-linear activation**.
- Understanding MLPs and activation functions is learning the **core language of deep learning**.
