understanding how neural networks generalize. When we train a neural network, the goal is not just
to fit the data, but to fit it appropriately. A model that is too simple may fail to capture
important patterns in the data, while a model that is too complex may capture patterns that do not
generalize beyond the training set. Both these situations lead to poor generalization. So, the
key question is not whether the model fits the data, but how it fits the data.
Let us start with underfitting. Underfitting occurs when a model is too simple for the task. In this case,
the model is unable to capture the underlying structure of the data. As you can see in this image,

result, the model performs poorly on the training data and also poorly on unseen data. Common reasons for
underfitting include: Insufficient model capacity, overly restrictive assumptions, inadequate features, or excessive regularization. In essence, the model has not learned enough from the data. Another problem is overfitting, which occurs when a model is too complex for the data.

Now, we can clearly distinguish between these behaviors by comparing training and generalization
errors. In underfitting, both training error and generalization error are high. In a well-fit
model, both the errors are low. In overfitting, training error is low but generalization error is
high. This simple comparison provides a powerful diagnostic tool for understanding the model
behavior. Model capacity refers to the expressive power of a neural network.
Low-capacity models struggle to represent complex patterns and are prone to underfitting. High-capacity
models can represent very complex functions which increases the risk of overfitting, especially when
the data is limited or noisy. Importantly, the same model can underfit or overfit depending on the
amount and quality of data available. This highlights that underfitting and overfitting are not just
properties of the model but of the model and data combination. Now, let us summarize the main points of this video.
Underfitting and overfitting represent two different ways in which models can fail to generalize. Both arise from an imbalance
between model capacity and data. Understanding this balance is critical for building reliable neural networks. In the next video, we will formalize this idea using the bias-variance trade-off which provides a theoretical framework for understanding these behaviors. Thank you.
