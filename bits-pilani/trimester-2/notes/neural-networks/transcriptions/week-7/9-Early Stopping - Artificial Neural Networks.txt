As we have seen, overfitting often develops gradually as training continues. Even when
the training loss keeps on decreasing, the model may start to perform worse on unseen data. This
means that continuing the training beyond a certain point can actually harm generalization. Early
stopping addresses this issue by recognizing that more training is not always better.
The core idea of early stopping is straightforward. During training, we continuously monitor performance
on a validation set. When validation performance stops improving for a certain number of iterations
or epochs, training is stopped. The model parameters corresponding to the best validation performance
are then selected as the final model. The main idea here is to stop the learning
process before the learning process before the model starts to memorize the noise in the data. Early stopping acts
as a form of regularization. By stopping training early, we limit how complex the learned model can become. This prevents weights from growing too large and reduces the tendency of the model to memorize noise in the training data. In effect, early stopping biases the model
towards simpler solutions that generalize better. In practice, early stopping is often visualized
and the training and validation curves.
Validation loss typically decreases steadily as training progresses. Validation loss, on the other hand, decreases initially, reaches a minimum and then starts to increase as overfitting sets in. Early stopping selects the model parameters corresponding to this minimum validation loss. When applying early stopping, several practical considerations are important. A patient's parameter is often used to avoid stopping early.
When applying early stopping, several practical considerations are important. A patient's
parameter is often used to avoid stopping early due to minor fluctuations in validation performance.
It is also common to restore the model weights from the epoch with the best validation performance.
Finally, early stopping works best when the validation set is representative of the data
the model will encounter in practice. Now, let us summarize the main points of this video.
Early stopping is a simple yet powerful way to prevent overfitting during training.
It requires no changes to the model architecture and can be combined effectively with other regularization techniques.
