Training deep neural networks can be challenging. As training progresses, the distributions of
activations in different layers can shift, even when earlier layers change only slightly. This
makes optimization harder, slows down convergence, and increases sensitivity to hyperparameters such
as the learning rate and initialization. Batch normalization was introduced to address this
instability during the training. The core idea behind batch normalization is to normalize layer
inputs within each mini-batch. Specifically, activations are centered to have zero mean and
scale to have unit variance. By keeping activations in a stable range, batch normalization makes training
more predictable and helps gradients propagate more smoothly through the network. Now, let's briefly
look at the mathematical formulation. For each mini-batch of activations, we compute the mean and the variance. And then, each activation is basically centered by subtracting the mean and dividing by the variance with a small epsilon for numerical stability.
This normalization step ensures that the distribution of activations is consistent across training iterations. Importantly, batch normalization does not restrict the expressive power of the network. Since different mini-batches have slightly different statistics, this introduces noise
into the training process. This noise acts as a form of implicit regularization, similar in spirit
to dropout but arising naturally from the normalization process. In practice, batch
normalization offers several advantages. It allows the use of higher learning rates,
reduces sensitivity to initialization and often speeds up the convergence process.
Because of its stabilizing effect, batch normalization can also reduce the need for
other regularization techniques in some settings. Now, let us summarize the main points of this video.
Batch normalization stabilizes activations during training, making the optimization easier and more
reliable. At the same time, the noise introduced by batch statistics often leads to improved
