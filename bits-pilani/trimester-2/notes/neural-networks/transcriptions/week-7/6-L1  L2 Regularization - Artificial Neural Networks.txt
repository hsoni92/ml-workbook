Mathematically, regularization is introduced directly into the objective function. Instead
of minimizing only the data loss, we minimize a regularized loss that includes a penalty for
model complexity. The total loss is written as the data loss plus the regularization term which is scaled
by the parameter lambda. The parameter lambda controls the trade-off between fitting the data well and keeping the model simple. A larger lambda places more emphasis on simplicity while a smaller lambda prioritizes data fit. Now, let us start with L2 regularization, also known as weight decay. In L2 regularization, the penalty term is the sum of the score.
values of the model weights. This means that large weights are penalized more heavily than
smaller ones. As a result, the optimization process naturally favors solutions where weights
are small and distributed across many features. L2 regularization is widely used in deep learning
because it leads to smooth stable models and improves generalization. Now, let us try to

L2 regularization reduces the influence of any single parameter, making the model less sensitive to noise in the data. This leads to smoother decision boundaries and more stable predictions on unseen data.
Next comes L1 regularization. L1 regularization takes a slightly different approach. Here, the penalty term is the sum of the absolute values of the weights, as we can see here. This one.
Because of this absolute value penalty, it encourages sparsity. During training, many weights are driven exactly to zero, effectively removing some features from the model. Now, let's look at it geometrically. In contrast to L2, L1 regularization has a diamond-shaped constraint region with sharp corners on the axis. So, this is the region that we are talking about.
This geometry makes it highly probable that the intersection point will fall directly on a corner, forcing some weights to become exactly zero, thereby performing feature selection. From a mathematical perspective, the key difference between L1 and L2 lies in the shape of their penalty functions.
In contrast, L2 regularization has a smooth quadratic penalty, which leads to gradual shrinking of weights, while L1 regularization has sharp corners due to the absolute value, which causes some weights to collapse exactly to zero.
Because of these properties, L2 is more commonly used in deep neural networks, while L1 is often used when sparsity is desired.
