In practice, convolutional neural networks are not built by applying isolated operations. Instead, they are constructed by repeatedly stacking a small set of operations together. A typical convolutional block starts with a convolution using multiple filters
This is followed by a non-linear activation and often includes design choices such as stride, padding and pooling. By repeating this simple block many times, CNNs gradually transform raw inputs into rich hierarchical representation. Let us now understand the role played by each component in this block. The convolution operation is responsible for extracting patterns and increasing the depth of the representation.
