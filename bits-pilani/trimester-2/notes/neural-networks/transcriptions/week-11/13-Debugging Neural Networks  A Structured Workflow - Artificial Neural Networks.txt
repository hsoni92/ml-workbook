Professional machine learning engineers do not guess. They don't keep changing hyperparameters randomly. They look inside the model and measure what is happening. Specifically, they inspect gradients, activations, and parameters because these tell you how learning is actually happening. These three signals form the backbone of neural network debugging. Gradients tell us whether learning signals are flowing. Activations tell us whether neurons are alive and responsive.
Parameters tell us whether the model is actually changing. Every failure mode we have seen in this module shows up in at least one of these three. This table shows how symptoms map to diagnostics.
