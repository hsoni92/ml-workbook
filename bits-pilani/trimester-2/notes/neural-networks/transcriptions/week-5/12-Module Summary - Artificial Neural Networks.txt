Hello everyone. Welcome to the last video of module 5 of Artificial Neural Networks.
So, what did we learn this week? In this module, you learn how the forward pass produces predictions
and how backpropagation computes gradients using the chain rule. You saw that gradients depend
directly on the loss function, which is why MSE and cross-entropy behave so differently in practice.
We then explored gradient flow in deep networks and understood why repeated multiplication of
local derivatives can make the gradients either vanish or explode. To address this, we studied
practical techniques like Xavier and He initialization, Relu family activations, gradient clipping,
normalization layers. These tools form the core of modern deep learning practice. By now,
you should have a clear understanding not only of how the neural networks learn, but also of why
certain architectures and training techniques are necessary for stable and efficient learning.
In the next module, we move from how gradients behave to how we optimize them, exploring optimizers like
momentum, RMS prop, ADAM, learning rate scheduling, and the practical dynamics of training deep networks.
These concepts will help you debug, speed up, and stabilize training in real-world applications.
See you all in the next module. Thank you.
