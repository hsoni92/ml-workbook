Bucket policies & iam permissions transcript

In this topic, we are going to understand how access to S3 buckets is actually
controlled because this is one of the most important and commonly misunderstood
parts of AWS storage security.

There are two main layers involved in this, IAM permissions and bucket policies.
They sound similar, but they solve different problems.

Let's start with IAM permissions, something which we have already covered in the
earlier topics. IAM permissions are identity based. They answer a simple question,
what is this user role or service allowed to do? For example, an EC2 instance might
need permission to read and write objects in a specific S3 bucket.

So in these cases, permissions are attached to the role, not the bucket, because the
role is something that will help to bridge the gap between this to perform the
operation.

IAM works very well for internal AWS access because it is centralised and reusable.
Now let's talk about bucket policies.

Bucket policies are resource based. Instead of asking who are you, they ask what
rules apply to this bucket. With bucket policies, you can control access based on
things like which AWS account is calling, where the request is coming from, or
whether encryption is being used or not.

This is commonly used for cross account access or restricting access to a particular
VPC also. Now here's the most important part. Both IAM and bucket policies are
evaluated together.

For any S3 request to succeed, an IAM policy must allow it. The bucket policy must
allow it and there must be no explicit deny anywhere. If there's even one explicit
deny, access will fail.

That is why people often see access denied errors even when IAM permissions look
absolutely correct. The issue is the bucket policy is blocking it. A simple way to
remember this is IAM permissions is like your ID card.

Bucket policies are like the building's security rules. Even if you have an ID card, you
still can't enter if the building doesn't allow you in. Or you can take an example of a
get to buy or top up your metro pass and when there's no balance you cannot
proceed to the platform.
