Pricing model & cost advantages transcript

Let's talk about one of the biggest reasons why serverless became so popular. It's
pricing model. Unlike traditional servers where you pay even when nothing is
happening, serverless completely flips the idea of cost.

With AWS Lambda, you only pay when your cloud actually runs. If nothing runs, you
pay nothing. That single idea already makes serverless very attractive option.

Let's break down how Lambda pricing really works in simple terms. There are only
three things that affect your cost. First is the number of requests.

Every time your function is triggered, it counts as one request. AWS even gives a
generous free tier here, which makes experimentation very easy for you. Second is
execution time.

You are charged based on how long your function runs. It is measured in milliseconds.
The faster your function finishes, the cheaper it becomes.

Third is memory allocation. When you assign more memory, AWS also gives more
CPU power. That means your function may run faster even though memory is higher.

This creates an interesting effect. Sometimes giving more memory actually reduces
total cost. A helpful way to understand serverless pricing is think of EC2 like renting a
car for the whole day.

Even if you drive only 10 minutes, you still pay for 24 hours. Lambda is like using a
taxi. You pay only when you are actually riding in the cab.

If you rarely travel, the taxi is much cheaper for you. If you drive nonstop all day,
owning a car might make more sense. This is why serverless is perfect for workloads
that are event driven, unpredictable, or idle most of the time.

And these type of scenarios could commonly lead to background jobs, notifications,
processing files, or any other automation task. You don't need to guess the capacity.
You don't overpay for unused servers.

Everything scales automatically. Another major advantage is that you don't pay for
infrastructure management also. There is no cost for patching servers, upgrading OS,
managing capacity, or handling the scaling logic.

AWS handles all of that for you. However, serverless is not always the cheapest
option. As mentioned earlier, if your workload runs continuously, processes heavy

Serverless pricing is pay -per-use, automatic, scalable, and beginner -friendly. It

systems.
