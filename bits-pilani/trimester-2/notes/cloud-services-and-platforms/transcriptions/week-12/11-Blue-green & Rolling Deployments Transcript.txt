Blue-green & Rolling Deployments Transcript

In this topic, we are going to understand two very important deployment strategies
used in DevOps and cloud environments, blue-green deployment and rolling updates.
These strategies define how new versions of applications are released without
disrupting the user experience. Let's start with blue-green deployment.

In a blue -green setup, you maintain two identical environments. One environment
called blue, which is currently serving the users, where the users are actually
navigating and going through the application, where the traffic is being served. The
other, green.

This is where the new version gets deployed, which no one has access to. Once the
new version in the green is tested and verified, traffic is switched from blue to the green
using a load balancer or a routing rule. If something goes wrong, you can immediately
switch traffic back to the blue.

This makes rollback extremely fast and safe. That's why blue-green deployments are
often used for critical systems where the failure or bugs are unacceptable. The trade-
off here is the cost.

Because you are temporarily running two full environments, it will involve some cost.
Now, let's talk about rolling deployments. In a rolling deployment, updates happen
gradually.

Instead of switching traffic all at once, the system updates one instance at a time. An
old instance or an old server is taken out of service, updated, checked, and then
brought back again. This process repeats until all the instances are updated.

Rolling deployments are very popular because they don't require duplicate
environments and work naturally with the auto -scaling groups and also Kubernetes.
However, during the update, some users may hit the old version while others hit the
new one. This means backward compatibility becomes very important.

Rolling updates are efficient and cost -friendly and also widely used, especially in
container platforms. To summarise, blue -green deployments prioritise safety and
instant rollback at the cost of extra infrastructure. Rolling deployments prioritise
efficiency and simplicity but require a careful version compatibility.

Choosing the right deployment strategy directly impacts reliability, user experience,
and confidence in your release process. Because at the end of the day, whatever
application that we are trying to launch to the customers, that should not affect or
hinder their experience.
