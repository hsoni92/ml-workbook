What is DevOps Transcript

Let's start by understanding what DevOps really means because it's often
misunderstood. DevOps is not just a tool and it is not just a job title. DevOps is a way
of working that brings development and operation teams together so that the software
can be delivered faster and more reliably.

Traditionally, developers focused on writing the code and the operation teams focused
on keeping the systems stable and running. These goals often conflicted a lot.
Developers wanted speed and operations wanted a better stability and this created
the friction between them.

So DevOps emerged to solve that problem. It focusses on collaboration, automation,
and shared responsibility. Now, let's talk about the culture which is actually the most
important part of DevOps.

A DevOps culture encourages teams to work together and everyone has proper
ownership of their respective outcomes. Failures are treated as learning opportunities,
not the reason to blame the individuals. So a common DevOps mindset is you build it,
you run it.

Next comes collaboration. DevOps teams don't work in isolation. Developers, testers,
operations, and even security teams collaborate continuously.

They use shared tools, dashboards, and workflows so everyone has the visibility into
what's happening. Then we have automation, the engine that makes DevOps scalable.
So automation removes repetitive manual work like building the code, running your
tests, deploying applications, and provisioning infrastructure.

This reduces human errors and allows teams to move much faster without sacrificing
their reliability. Cloud platforms play a big role here. They provide APIs, manage
services, and built-in automation that makes the DevOps practises at scale.

That is why DevOps and cloud usually go hand in hand. A simple real -world analogy
is a modern restaurant kitchen. The chefs prepare the dishes, staff coordinate the
orders, and the machines help automate repetitive tasks.

Everyone works together to deliver the quality food quickly. DevOps works the same
way for a software delivery. When the DevOps is done right, organisations release
faster, recover quickly from failures, collaborate better, and also deliver more value to
the users.

So DevOps play a very important role for a software to be released or any product that
is to be released to the customers.
