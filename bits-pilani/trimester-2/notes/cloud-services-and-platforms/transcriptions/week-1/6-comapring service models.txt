In previous topic, we understood what Infra, Platform and Software-as-a-Service are.
Now let's take it step further and compare these service models based on
responsibilities and use cases. This comparison will help you understand who
manages what and why different companies choose different models.
Let's start with the core idea. When you use cloud services, both you and the cloud
provider share responsibilities. But how much responsibility you have completely
depends on whether you are using Infra, Platform or Software-as-a-Service.Let's
break down the responsibility layers. Imagine a stack with these components.
Networking, Servers, Storage, Virtualization, OS, Runtime, Application and Data.
Now watch how responsibilities shifts. In Infra-as-a-Service, the cloud provider
manages the hardware, the network, the storage and virtualization layer. But you, the
customer, manage everything above that.
The operating system, the runtime, your application and your data. This means you
have maximum control but also the most responsibility. Let's look into it from a reallife perspective.
Companies migrating legacy systems that require custom OS configurations often
choose Infra-as-a-Service. In Platform-as-a-Service, the provider manages more
layers. The hardware, the virtualization, the operating system, the runtime and even
scaling.
You only manage your application code and your data. This makes Platform-as-aService great for developers who want to build and deploy quickly without worrying
about server management. Startups focusing on fast development cycles like
commonly choose Platform-as-a-Service.In Software-as-a-Service, the provider
manages everything. Infra, Platform, Application itself, everything. You only use the
software.
This gives you zero maintenance and maximum convenience. Tools like Gmail, Zoom,
Salesforce, all of them fall under Software-as-a-Service because customers just log in
and use them. Let's simplify this with a simple analogy, the one which we commonly
take, the most famous example by now, the housing example.
Think of it like three types of living arrangements. Infra-as-a-Service is like renting an
empty apartment. You get the structure but you take care of everything
inside.Platform-as-a-Service is like renting a semi-furnished house. The furniture and
basic setup are there so you can start living quickly. Software-as-a-Service is like
staying in a hotel.
Everything is managed for you. You simply enjoy the service. Each option has its
purpose depending on what you need.
