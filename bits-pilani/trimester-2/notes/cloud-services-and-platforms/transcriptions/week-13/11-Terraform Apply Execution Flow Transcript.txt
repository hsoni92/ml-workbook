Terraform Apply Execution Flow Transcript

When we run Terraform Apply, a lot more happens behind the scenes than just
creating resources. Understanding this flow is very important because it helps you
debug issues and trust the automation. First, Terraform reads all your configuration
files, which is your main.tf, variables, and provide rs, and builds an internal picture of
what you want.

At this stage, nothing is created yet. It's just updating and understanding its intent.
Next, Terraform refreshes the state.

This means it checks what already exists in the cloud and compares that with what it
believes exists in the state file. Basically, it is looking for a comparison between the
actual and the desired state. If someone manually deleted or modified something,
Terraform notices it from here.

After that, Terraform creates an execution plan. This plan is basically a preview. It
shows what will be created, updated, or destroyed.

This is a safety feature. Nothing changes yet. Then comes the confirmation step.

Terraform pauses and waits for your approval. This is your last checkpoint before real
infrastructure changes happen in your cloud environment. Once you approve,
Terraform starts applying the changes one by one.

It talks directly to AWS APIs and creates resources in the correct order. Dependencies
are respected automatically. For example, a subnet is created before an EC2 instance.

You'll see live logs showing what is being created, updated, or completed. These logs
are helpful when something is slow or fails. After everything finishes successfully,
Terraform updates the state file.

This file becomes the source of truth for your future runs. If something fails in the
middle, Terraform stops immediately. Some resources may already exist, but the next
run will reconcile the difference instead of starting from scratch.

A simple way to think about Terraform Apply is like placing an online order. You review
the cart, confirm the payment, items ship one by one, and your order history updates
at the end. Updating this execution flow makes Terraform much less scary and more
predictable, especially when used in the automation and the CI-CD pipelines.

Let's also try to understand this exact flow from a practical point of view.
