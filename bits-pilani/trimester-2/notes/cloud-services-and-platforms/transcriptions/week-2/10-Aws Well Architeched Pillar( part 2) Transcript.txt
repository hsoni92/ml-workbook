In this lesson, we'll cover the final three pillars of well -architected framework.
Operational excellence, performance efficiency, and sustainability. These pillars focus
on how cloud systems are operated, optimized, and sustained over time.
Let's start with operational excellence. Operational excellence is about running
systems efficiently and improving them continuously. It emphasizes automation over
manual work because manual processes don't scale and are prone to errors.
Automation is applied to infrastructure provisioning, backups, or even for recovery. So
when the systems are automated, teams spend less time fixing the issues and more
time improving the platform. Another core concept is observability.
Cloud systems must expose metrics, logs, and traces so the teams can understand
system behavior in real time. Without observability, troubleshooting becomes more of
a guesswork. So operational excellence also assumes failures will happen.
The focus is on fast detection, effective response, and learning through post -incident
reviews so that the same issue doesn't repeat. Now let's move on to performance
efficiency. The goal is to focus on using the right resources to meet workload needs,
not more, not less.
It's not about maximum performance but appropriate performance. This includes
selecting the correct compute storage and networking options, stateless services,
caching, and asynchronous processing. All of these improve performance without
unnecessary resource usage.
So performance efficiency also encourages experimentation. Architects test different
configurations, measure results, and continuously optimize as the workloads evolve.
Managed services play a bigger role here.
They often deliver better performance with less operational overhead. Finally, let's look
at the sustainability pillar focuses on reducing the environmental impact of cloud
workloads. Over-provisioning and ideal resources consume energy without delivering
value.
So efficient architectures, especially auto-scaling and serverless models, these ensure
resources are used only when needed. A data lifecycle management also matters.
Archiving or deleting unused data reduces long-time storage waste.
Sustainability is increasingly important as organizations track carbon impact alongside
cost and performance. So to summarize, operational excellence ensures systems are
run and improved effectively. Performance efficiency ensures resources are used
widely as the systems scale.
