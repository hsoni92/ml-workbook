Aws Well Architeched Pillar (Part 1 )Transcript
In this lesson, we are going to focus on three of the most critical pillars of the well -
architected framework, security, reliability, and cost optimization. These three pillars
directly affect risk, uptime, and cloud spend, which is why architects and busi ness
leaders care deeply about them. Let's start with the security pillar.
The security pillar is about protecting data, systems, and identities in the cloud. In
cloud environments, security follows a shared responsibility model. They provide
securely underlying infrastructure, while the customers are responsible for securing
what they deploy on top of it.
A strong security design starts with identity. Access should always follow the principle
of least privilege. Users and services should only have the permission they truly need.
Data production is another core aspect. Sensitive data must be encrypted both when
it is stored and when it is moving across networks. Managed key services help ensure
encryption keys are protected and rotated safely.
Infrastructure protection also plays a major role. This includes network isolation,
private endpoints, firewalls, and protection against common attacks like DDoS. Finally,
security is incomplete without detection and response.
Logs, monitoring, and alerts allow teams to detect unusual activity early and respond
quickly. Security is stronger when it is built into the architecture from the beginning
rather than later on. Reliability pillar, let's look into this.
It is the ability of a system to continue operating correctly even when the components
fail. In the cloud, failure is expected. So, reliable architectures use redundancy,
automation, and recovery strategies.
So, change management is also critical. Automated deployments, infrastructure as
code, and controlled rollouts all of this reduce the risk of outages caused by human
error. Two important reliability metrics are RTO and RPO.
RTO defines how quickly the system must recover and RPO defines how much data
loss is acceptable. These values are driven by business requirements, not just
technical preferences. Cost optimization pillar, finally, we have the cost optimization
pillar which ensures that cloud resources are used effectively and efficiently so that
the money is not wasted.
A key strategy is right sizing. Over provisioning may feel safe but it leads to
unnecessary cost. Demand-based scaling helps control the cost.
So, when the demand drops, you can reduce it. When the demand raises, you can
increase it. Cloud providers also often offer different pricing models which can
significantly help you to reduce the cost when you're using it correctly.

Storage optimization, another major area where you're moving your older data to a
cheaper storage to reduce some amount of money over the time. So, cloud
optimization is not one-time exercise. Strong security reduces the risk.
High reliability protects revenue and user trust. Smart cost optimization ensures a
long-term sustainability. So, effective cloud architecture is not about maximizing one
pillar.
It's about balancing all these three. In the next lesson, we are going to continue with
