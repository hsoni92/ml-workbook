Rds resilience & backups transcript

Now, let's talk about one of the most important reliable features in the cloud databases,
multi-AZ deployments, along with automated backups and snapshots. These three
ideas often get mixed up. So let's clearly separate what each one does.

First, let's understand multi-AZ deployments. Multi-AZ means your database is running
in more than one availability zone inside the same region. There is a primary database
that handles traffic and a standby database sitting in another zone.

The key idea here is to protect from failure. If something goes wrong, hardware issue,
power issue, or network outage, AWS will automatically switch the traffic from the
primary to the standby. This happens without manual intervention.

Your application continues running with minimal disruption. Now, here's an important
clarification. Multi-AZ is not designed to make your database faster.

It does not help with scaling reads. Its only job is availability and reliability. The
distinction is very important and often misunderstood.

Next, let's talk about automated backups. Automated backups help you recover from
mistakes, not outages. AWS takes a full daily backup, continuous transaction logs.

This allows point-in-time recovery, meaning you can restore your database to almost
any second within your retention window. If someone accidentally deletes data or runs
a bad query, backups will save you. Retention typically ranges from one to three, five
days.

Now, let's talk about snapshots, which are a bit different. Snapshots are manually
backups that you take intentionally. They are stored until you delete them and can also
be copied to other regions, shared with other accounts, used to create new databases.

So, snapshots are commonly used for long -term storage, compliance, disaster
recovery, and environment cloning also. Here's an easy real -world way to remember
this. Think of a bank.

Multi-AZ is like having a second live data centre ready instantly. Automated backups
are like CCTV recordings you can rewind. Snapshots are like archived system images
kept for audits.

Each serves a different purpose. So, to summarise, Multi -AZ keeps your system
running during the failures. Automated backups help you recover from mistakes.
