# What is Cloud Computing and Its Key Traits

## Definition
Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services over the internet on a pay-as-you-use basis. These services include compute, storage, databases, networking, analytics, AI tools, and more.

## Utility Computing Mindset
Cloud is often described as a utility model (similar to electricity or water):
- Consume when needed
- Pay for actual usage
- Avoid owning and maintaining all infrastructure

## Core Traits of Cloud

### 1) Scalability
Ability to increase resources to handle growing demand.
- Example: Website traffic jumps from hundreds to lakhs of users.
- Benefit: Prevents outages and performance collapse during growth.

### 2) Elasticity
Ability to scale up and scale down dynamically based on real-time demand.
- Example: E-commerce traffic spikes during festive sales, then drops.
- Benefit: Cost optimization through right-sized resource use.

### 3) On-Demand Self-Service
Users can provision resources instantly without long procurement cycles.
- Earlier model: ticket -> approval -> procurement -> setup (days/weeks)
- Cloud model: provision in minutes through console/API
- Benefit: Speed and operational agility

## Why These Traits Matter Together
When combined, scalability + elasticity + on-demand provisioning provide:
- Better user experience under variable load
- Lower infrastructure waste
- Faster business response to market opportunities

## Real-World Illustration
Ride-booking platforms face peak and off-peak cycles:
- Peak hours: scale up compute and databases
- Late-night low traffic: scale down
- Outcome: lower cost and consistent app performance

## Important Distinction
- **Scalability:** capability to grow capacity
- **Elasticity:** automatic and dynamic adjustment of capacity

## Quick Revision
- Cloud = internet-delivered IT resources + usage-based pricing.
- Core traits: scalability, elasticity, on-demand self-service.
- These traits are key reasons cloud outperforms traditional static infrastructure.
