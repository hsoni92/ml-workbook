# Choosing the Right Cloud Model

## Objective
Build a practical decision framework to choose among public, private, hybrid, and multi-cloud.

## Four Primary Decision Factors

### 1) Cost and Budget
- Public cloud: lower upfront cost, usage-based pricing, minimal hardware ownership.
- Private cloud: higher capital and operational investment, suited to large enterprises needing full control.

### 2) Security and Compliance
- Strictly regulated sectors may require private or hybrid models.
- Hybrid helps keep sensitive data private while using public cloud for scalable components.

### 3) Performance and Scalability Needs
- Public cloud is strong for unpredictable traffic spikes and global delivery.
- Private cloud can be constrained by owned infrastructure limits.
- Hybrid supports cloud bursting for peak demand.

### 4) Vendor Strategy and Technical Fit
- Multi-cloud helps avoid over-dependence on a single provider.
- Teams can choose provider-specific strengths (compute, AI/ML, integrations, regional advantages).
- Multi-cloud can improve outage resilience by workload distribution.

## Practical Selection Guide
- Choose **public cloud** for speed, low entry cost, and rapid scale.
- Choose **private cloud** for strict control and high-sensitivity workloads.
- Choose **hybrid cloud** when both governance and elasticity are required.
- Choose **multi-cloud** for flexibility, redundancy, and best-of-provider services.

## Example Scenarios
- Startup launching nationwide app quickly -> Public cloud.
- Bank with strict compliance controls -> Private or Hybrid.
- Enterprise with mixed workload sensitivity -> Hybrid.
- Global digital company minimizing lock-in risk -> Multi-cloud.

## Decision Mistakes to Avoid
- Selecting architecture only by cost, ignoring compliance and operations.
- Assuming one model fits every workload in an organization.
- Ignoring long-term portability and vendor dependence.

## Exam-Focused Preparation
- Memorize the four decision factors and map each to a model.
- Be ready to recommend a model for a short business case.
- Explain why hybrid and multi-cloud can coexist in mature organizations.

## Quick Revision
- Cloud model selection is a business + security + technical decision.
- Correct choice depends on workload profile, regulation, and growth strategy.
