Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs): Specialized for processing grid-like data such as images.
They use convolutional layers to detect features.
Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs): Designed to work with sequential data. They have connections
that form directed cycles, allowing them to maintain memory of previous inputs.
8. Challenges and Limitations
- Need for large amounts of training data
- Computational complexity and resource requirements
- Black box nature - difficult to interpret decisions
- Risk of overfitting to training data
- Vulnerability to adversarial attacks
9. Future Directions
The field of neural networks continues to evolve rapidly. Current research focuses on developing
more efficient architectures, improving interpretability, reducing computational requirements, and
creating more robust models that can generalize better to unseen data.
Emerging areas include attention mechanisms, transformer architectures, and neural networks that
can learn with less supervision. These developments are pushing the boundaries of what's possible
with artificial intelligence.
10. Summary
Neural networks represent a powerful approach to machine learning that has revolutionized many
fields. By mimicking the structure and function of biological neural networks, they can learn complex
patterns and relationships in data.
